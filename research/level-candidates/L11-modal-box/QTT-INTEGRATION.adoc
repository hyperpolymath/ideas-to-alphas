[abstract]
Resolves the open item "QTT integration: formally define how Box interacts with
QTT multiplicities" (`MOTIVATION.adoc`, open item 2). The answer is *graded
necessity*: replace the ungraded `Box` of `Modal.idr` with `□_r A` indexed by a
grade drawn from L10's multiplicity semiring, and derive `dup` / `unbox` /
`release` from the semiring operations rather than postulating them.

toc::[]

== Scope

This document is *normative for the L11 candidate only*. It does not change L10,
and it does not graduate anything to `typell`. It fixes the typing rules that a
graded `Box` must satisfy, and records one consequence that invalidates the
current `Modal.idr` sketch (<<dup-theorem>>).

== 1. Two grades, not one

The single most important thing to get right before any rule is written: there are
*two distinct graded structures* in this estate, over *different semirings*, and
they must not be conflated.

[cols="1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| | *Usage grade* (this document) | *Disclosure grade* (`Echo`)

| Question answered
| How many times may this resource be consumed?
| How much of this secret may be recovered?

| Semiring
| Multiplicity `R = {0, 1, ω}` (L10; `Modal.idr`'s `Multiplicity`)
| Tropical / min-plus `(ℕ ∪ {∞}, min, +)`

| Zero / One
| `0` = erased, `1` = linear
| `∞` = nothing recoverable, `0` = fully recoverable

| Carrier in the estate
| `typell` L10; `Modal.idr`
| `epistemic-types/src/EpistemicTypes/EchoBridge.agda` (`gradeMin`, `gradePlus`)

| Proved where
| Not yet (this is the gap)
| `trope-checker/spec/calculus-v0.2-draft.adoc` (Lean 4)

| Endpoints
| `0` erased … `ω` unrestricted
| `r=0` RMR (reversible) … `r=∞` RMO (obliterated)
|===

Both `□_r A` (usage) and `S ℓ A` / `Echo r A` (disclosure) are *graded comonads* —
`epistemic-types/docs/secret-types.adoc` says so explicitly of `S ℓ A` ("graded
comonad / coeffect graded by clearance"). That structural similarity is exactly
what makes the conflation tempting and wrong: *same shape, different semiring,
different theorem*. `ω + ω = ω` is idempotence of unrestricted use; `min(∞,∞) = ∞`
is idempotence of total obliteration. They are not the same fact.

[IMPORTANT]
====
L11 is graded by *usage* only. Disclosure grading is orthogonal and belongs to the
silo / `epistemic-types` line. If both are ever needed on one type, the result is
*bigraded* — `□_r (S ℓ A)` — not a single grade over a fused semiring. Fusing them
would silently identify "used twice" with "leaked twice", which is false.
====

== 2. The usage semiring

Let `R = (R, +, ·, 0, 1, ⊑)` be an ordered semiring. For L10, `R = {0, 1, ω}`:

.Addition (how uses combine when a resource is *split*)
[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| `+` | `0` | `1` | `ω`
| `0` | `0` | `1` | `ω`
| `1` | `1` | `ω` | `ω`
| `ω` | `ω` | `ω` | `ω`
|===

.Multiplication (how uses compose when a resource is *nested*)
[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| `·` | `0` | `1` | `ω`
| `0` | `0` | `0` | `0`
| `1` | `0` | `1` | `ω`
| `ω` | `0` | `ω` | `ω`
|===

`⊑` is the subsumption preorder: `r ⊑ s` means a resource graded `s` may stand
where grade `r` is required. `ω` is top (`0 ⊑ ω`, `1 ⊑ ω`); `0` and `1` are
*incomparable* — an erased value cannot be used once, and a linear value cannot be
silently discarded.

[NOTE]
====
`R` is deliberately presented as a parameter, not hardcoded. The rules below hold
for any ordered semiring, so L11 extends to `ℕ ∪ {ω}` (exact-count grades) without
re-derivation. Pin the concrete choice against `typell/src/abi/TypedWasm/ABI/Levels.idr`
before graduation — Idris 2's own `{0,1,ω}` ordering conventions differ from the
presentation here and the mismatch must be checked, not assumed.
====

== 3. Graded contexts

Contexts carry a grade per variable: `Γ = x₁ :^{r₁} A₁, …, xₙ :^{rₙ} Aₙ`.

* *Scaling*: `s · Γ = x₁ :^{s·r₁} A₁, …, xₙ :^{s·rₙ} Aₙ`
* *Addition*: `(Γ + Δ)(x) = Γ(x) + Δ(x)`, pointwise over a shared variable set
* *Subsumption*: `Γ ⊑ Δ` pointwise

== 4. The rules

=== Promotion (introduction)

[stem]
++++
\frac{\Gamma \vdash a : A}{r \cdot \Gamma \vdash \mathsf{box}_r\ a : \Box_r A}
++++

To build a box usable at grade `r`, *every free variable it captures is scaled by
`r`*. This is the rule that does the real work, and it is why graded necessity does
not need S4's "context must be empty/all-boxed" restriction: a box may capture
locals provided the context can *afford* them `r` times.

=== Extract (elimination at 1)

[stem]
++++
\frac{\Gamma \vdash b : \Box_r A \qquad 1 \sqsubseteq r}{\Gamma \vdash \mathsf{unbox}\ b : A}
++++

This *resolves* `MOTIVATION.adoc`'s informal open item ("the grade of `unbox`
should be ≤ the grade of the enclosing context"). Stated precisely: extraction
happens at grade `1`, and is admissible exactly when `1 ⊑ r` — i.e. `r ∈ {1, ω}`.
At `r = 0` the content is erased and `unbox` is *illegal*, not merely discouraged.

=== Comultiplication (nesting — grade multiplies)

[stem]
++++
\frac{\Gamma \vdash b : \Box_{r \cdot s} A}{\Gamma \vdash \delta\ b : \Box_r (\Box_s A)}
++++

`Modal.idr`'s `boxLift` / `boxFlatten` pair is the ungraded shadow of this rule.
Note `□□A ≅ □A` (the S4 idempotence the sketch claims) is *only* an isomorphism
when `r · s = r = s`, which in `R` means `r = s ∈ {0, ω}` — see below.

=== Split (sharing — grade adds)

[stem]
++++
\frac{\Gamma \vdash b : \Box_{r + s} A}{\Gamma \vdash \mathsf{split}\ b : \Box_r A \otimes \Box_s A}
++++

*This, not duplication, is the correct rule for `dup`.* Sharing a box divides its
grade between the two halves; it does not replicate it.

=== Discard (weakening at 0)

[stem]
++++
\frac{\Gamma \vdash b : \Box_0 A}{\Gamma \vdash \mathsf{drop}\ b : I}
++++

Weakening is admissible only at grade `0`.

== 5. Consequence: the current `dup` pins the box to ω [[dup-theorem]]

`Modal.idr` declares:

[source,idris]
----
dup : Box a -> (Box a, Box a)
dup (MkBox x) = (MkBox x, MkBox x)
----

Read graded, this is `dup : □_r A ⊸ □_r A ⊗ □_r A` — input and both outputs at the
*same* grade `r`.

[NOTE]
.Proposition
====
If `dup : □_r A ⊸ □_r A ⊗ □_r A` is admissible, then `r + r = r`.
====

*Proof.* The only rule producing `□_r A ⊗ □_s A` is `split`, whose premise is
`□_{r+s} A`. Instantiating `s := r` gives premise `□_{r+r} A`; the declared input is
`□_r A`; so `r + r = r`. ∎

In `R = {0, 1, ω}`: `0 + 0 = 0` ✓, `1 + 1 = ω ≠ 1` ✗, `ω + ω = ω` ✓. Hence
`r ∈ {0, ω}`. Grade `0` is erased (`unbox` illegal by <<_extract_elimination_at_1>>),
so *any useful instance of the current `dup` forces `r = ω`*.

This is precisely the failure `MOTIVATION.adoc` anticipated in prose —

[quote]
____
"`dup` at L10 would require the resource to have multiplicity ω (unrestricted),
losing the management discipline."
____

— and it is now a checkable statement rather than a worry. The sketch does not
*avoid* that collapse; it *is* the `ω` instance of the graded system, with the
grade left implicit. The comonad laws proved in `Modal.idr` (`comonadLaw1`,
`comonadLaw2`) are sound but are the laws of the `ω`-instance only; they are not
evidence that the discipline is preserved at `r = 1`.

[WARNING]
====
This blocks graduation. `Modal.idr` must be rewritten so that `dup` splits the
grade before L11 can claim to add anything over L10's `ω`. As it stands, `Box a`
is isomorphic to "an L10 value at multiplicity ω" and L11 earns its level number
only once `split` replaces `dup`.
====

== 6. The linear handle vs. the graded content

`Modal.idr` conflates two obligations in one type:

. the *modality* — how many times may the content be read (`unbox`)?
. the *resource obligation* — the box itself must be `release`-d exactly once.

Only (1) is the graded comonad. (2) is linearity of the *handle*, and it is why
`release : Box a -> ()` is not the `drop` rule above: `drop` is weakening at grade
`0`, whereas `release` is a mandatory linear *action*. Keep them separate:

----
Box_r A   ≜   a handle, itself at multiplicity 1 in the ambient context,
              whose content is available at usage grade r
----

The pool example (`openPool` / `runQuery` / `closePool`) is then well-typed at
`r = ω` for reads with a linear handle for lifecycle — which is the intended
discipline, and which the graded reading makes explicit instead of implicit.

== 7. Relation to the `epistemic-types` line

* `S ℓ A` (`epistemic-types/docs/secret-types.adoc`) is a graded comonad over the
  *clearance lattice*, and its defining property — `BeliefModality` omits `reflect`
  — is the "you may carry it, you may `map` under it, you may not open it" rule.
  `□_r` is the *usage* analogue: `unbox` is `reflect`, and unlike `S ℓ A` we *do*
  admit it, gated on `1 ⊑ r`.
* `Echo r A` (`EchoBridge.agda`) grades *disclosure* over the tropical semiring and
  is the RMR/RMO axis. Orthogonal to `□_r`; compose as `□_r (S ℓ A)` if ever needed.
* `EchoBridge.agda` is flagged as *scaffold* by `epistemic-types/AUDIT.adoc:289` and
  `AFFIRMATION.adoc:207`. Treat it as a specification to agree with, *not* as a
  dependency to import.

== 8. What must change in `Modal.idr`

. Index `Box` by a grade: `Box : R -> Type -> Type`.
. Replace `dup` with `split : Box (r + s) a -> (Box r a, Box s a)`.
. Gate `unbox` on `1 ⊑ r`.
. Add `comult : Box (r * s) a -> Box r (Box s a)`; keep `boxFlatten` only as the
  `r · s = r` corollary.
. Prove the semiring laws for `Multiplicity` (currently a bare `data` with no
  algebraic content — no `+`, no `·`, no laws are defined on it at all).
. Prove `dupForcesOmega`: `(r : Multiplicity) -> r + r = r -> Either (r = Zero) (r = Many)`.
. Re-derive the comonad laws *at general `r`*, not at the implicit `ω`.

== 9. Open questions

. *Ordering convention.* Is `0 ⊑ 1`? This document says no (incomparable). Idris 2's
  own erasure semantics may effectively admit `0 ⊑ 1` at compile time. Must be pinned
  against `typell` L10 before graduation.
. *Is `R` a lattice or just an ordered semiring?* `MOTIVATION.adoc` says "TypeLL grade
  lattice"; `{0,1,ω}` with `0`,`1` incomparable and no bottom is *not* a lattice. Either
  the prose or the semiring is wrong.
. *WasmGC.* Does `□_ω` map to `table.get` and the linear handle to table-slot ownership?
  Unspecified (open item 3 in `MOTIVATION.adoc`).
. *Exact counts.* Does `ℕ ∪ {ω}` buy anything real for pools, or is `{0,1,ω}` enough?
